PiggyLog Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 24, 2026  ·  Version: 1.0

PiggyLog is a money diary for kids. This policy explains what information the app handles, where it lives, and what choices you have as a parent or guardian. It is written in plain language because we think the people using this app deserve plain answers.

If you have a question this policy doesn't answer, email us at piggylog.app@gmail.com.

1. The short version

PiggyLog v1 is offline-only. Everything your kid enters — earnings, purchases, allowance settings, the optional photo on an entry — stays on the device. None of it is sent to any server. We do not have servers in v1. We do not run analytics. We do not embed third-party trackers. We do not sell or share any of your data, because there is no data leaving the device for us to share.

That is the entire story in v1. Sections 2–8 are the long version.

2. What information PiggyLog handles

PiggyLog stores the following on the device:

  • Your kid's first name and avatar choice. Asked once during setup. Used to greet your kid on the Home screen. You can change this any time from Parent mode.
  • The transactions your kid logs. Earnings, allowance, and purchases — with amount, category, optional note, optional photo, and timestamp.
  • Your bucket-split preferences (Spend / Save / Give percentages).
  • Allowance settings: amount, day of month, on/off toggle.
  • Optional parent PIN, stored as a one-way hash so the PIN itself is not recoverable.
  • Audit history of parent actions (approvals, edits, deletes) so the parent can see what they changed.

We do not ask for or store:

  • Your kid's last name, school, birthday, address, or any other contact information.
  • Your kid's photo or face image. The optional photo on a transaction is meant to be a picture of the thing (the toy they bought, the chore they did), not of the kid.
  • Your kid's geolocation.
  • Any biometric information.

3. Where the information lives

Everything described in §2 is stored locally on the device, in the operating-system-protected app sandbox. PiggyLog v1 does not transmit any of this data over the internet. There is no PiggyLog server in v1.

If you uninstall PiggyLog, the operating system deletes all of this data. There is no copy anywhere else.

4. Third parties

PiggyLog v1 uses the following third-party software components, none of which transmit your data to their operators:

  • Expo / React Native — the framework the app is built on. Runs entirely on your device.
  • SQLite (via expo-sqlite) — the local database engine.
  • Lottie — for the on-screen animations. Reads animation files bundled inside the app; does not phone home.

We do not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or attribution SDKs in v1.

5. COPPA — Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (United States)

PiggyLog is designed for kids ages 8 and up, which means some of our users are under 13 and protected by COPPA.

We minimize children's personal information by design. The only information PiggyLog handles about a child is their first name and their own self-logged transaction entries (see §2), and this information never leaves the device. Because no personal information is collected by an "operator" in the COPPA sense (we have no servers, no logs, no third-party sharing in v1), the collection-and-use provisions of COPPA do not apply in the usual way.

Even so, we follow the spirit of COPPA:

  • Parental control. Setup is performed by a parent or guardian. PiggyLog asks you to confirm this when you first open the app.
  • No targeted advertising. PiggyLog v1 has no ads.
  • No social features. Kids cannot contact other kids or strangers through PiggyLog. There is no chat, no messaging, no profile browsing.
  • Easy deletion. Uninstalling the app deletes all child information immediately. No retention.

If PiggyLog adds cloud sync, accounts, or any feature that transmits child information off-device (planned for v1.1), we will update this policy and obtain verifiable parental consent before that feature activates for your household.

6. Your choices as a parent or guardian

  • Change anything: re-enter parent mode (with the PIN you set, if any) and edit your kid's name, the bucket split, the allowance settings, or any transaction.
  • Delete a single entry: from Parent mode → Review queue → Delete.
  • Delete everything: uninstall the app. There is no separate "factory reset" option in v1 because uninstalling already does it.
  • Request information about your data: you don't need to ask us because we don't have it — but if you would like a written confirmation, email piggylog.app@gmail.com.

7. Changes to this policy

If we materially change how PiggyLog handles your data — for example when accounts and sync ship in v1.1 — we will:

  • Update the Effective date at the top of this policy.
  • Bump the Version number.
  • Show the new policy in the app the next time you open it, before any new behavior takes effect.

Editorial changes (typo fixes, clearer wording) won't bump the version or trigger a re-prompt.

8. Contact

Questions, concerns, or requests: piggylog.app@gmail.com.

For complaints about how we handle children's data specifically, you can also contact the U.S. Federal Trade Commission via ftc.gov/coppa.

PiggyLog v1 is provided as-is to a small private beta and an initial public launch. If the operator structure changes (for example, if PiggyLog incorporates as an LLC), this policy and the Operator line in §8 will be updated and the version bumped.
